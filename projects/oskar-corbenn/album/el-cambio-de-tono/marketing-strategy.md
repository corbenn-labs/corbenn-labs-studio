## Objective

Design a post-release marketing strategy that increases discoverability, audience growth, and meaningful engagement for Oskar Corbenn through *El Cambio de Tono* — building long-term audience trust and future-release recognition without sacrificing authenticity or pursuing short-term viral tactics.

This strategy translates approved G2 research into an executable marketing system aligned with the Campaign Brief, Brand DNA, and Master Context. It does not prescribe specific captions, hooks, or creative assets; those belong to the Content Engine at G4–G6 after strategy approval.

**What blocks growth:** The project has discovery entry points (Shorts) but no conversion destination. **Identity fragmentation:** Three distinct YouTube surfaces appear in search — Oskar Corbenn channel, Topic auto-upload, and a secondary channel named "El Cambio de Tono." No Official Artist Channel (OAC) consolidates these. Title search for "El Cambio de Tono" collides with unrelated content (SonAMA Music).

## Campaign Objective

Introduce new listeners to the emotional world of Oskar Corbenn through *El Cambio de Tono*, convert initial curiosity into sustained audience relationships, and establish *Razones Para Quedarme* as a recognizable album-era brand — laying the foundation for discoverability, engagement, and future monetization without compromising artistic identity.

**Primary business goal (from Campaign Brief):** Increase visibility and discoverability while building long-term audience growth infrastructure.

**Campaign framing:** Position *El Cambio de Tono* as **Chapter 1** of *Razones Para Quedarme*, not an isolated single. This reduces cold-start friction and aligns with comparable success models (Noche Cero, Blucer).

---

## Primary Audience

**Core segment:** Spanish-speaking adults aged 25–45 who appreciate emotionally honest storytelling, reflective lyrics, and cinematic rock music.

**Psychographic profile:** People who have experienced love, relational change, uncertainty, personal growth, or emotional reconstruction. They value authenticity over hype and are willing to invest 3–4 minutes in a song when trust is established.

**Discovery behavior:** Likely to encounter content passively (YouTube Shorts feed, YouTube Music search) before converting to active followership (channel subscribe, Spotify save, return for future releases).

**Not the primary audience:** Trend-chasing Gen Z TikTok natives seeking 15-second meme formats. Pursuing this segment would contradict Brand DNA and campaign constraints.

## Primary Platform

**YouTube (artist-owned channel)**

**Rationale (verified evidence):**
- Only platform with confirmed artist-owned, brand-aligned content
- Shorts already show traction (959 views on story-hook Short)
- Campaign Brief explicitly targets YouTube views and watch time
- Supports both discovery (Shorts) and depth (long-form) within one ecosystem
- Cinematic visual identity (dark, rain, night) maps naturally to YouTube video formats

**Strategic role:** YouTube is the **hub** — not one channel among many. All other platforms feed into or extend from YouTube-established trust.

## Publishing Sequence

Strategy follows a **hub-first, funnel-second, expand-third** sequence. Phases overlap but priorities are strict.

### Phase 0 — Infrastructure (Week 1)

Fix discovery barriers before increasing content volume.

| Action | Owner | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Confirm OAC eligibility via DistroKid / YouTube | Leadership + ops | Consolidate Topic + artist channel identity |
| Audit and standardize naming: **"Oskar Corbenn — El Cambio de Tono"** across all metadata | Ops | Reduce title collision with SonAMA and generic results |
| Request DistroKid dashboard access (Spotify/Apple streams, territories) | Leadership | Unlock DSP KPI baselines |
| Confirm Instagram handle and TikTok status | Leadership | Enable or defer secondary platform planning |
| Confirm long-form MV / visualizer production status and timeline | Leadership | Lock Phase 1 schedule |
| Add cross-links: artist channel description → Topic track, YouTube Music, confirmed DSPs | Ops | Reduce split-identity friction |

### Phase 1 — Long-form anchor (Weeks 2–4)

Establish the conversion destination the research identified as the critical gap.

| Priority | Format | Strategic purpose |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **1** | Cinematic music video or visualizer (artist channel) | Subscriber conversion; watch time; replaces Topic-only discovery |
| **2** | "Historia detrás de la canción" long-form video | Brand trust; comment engagement; leverages `story.md` narrative depth |

**Rule:** No new Shorts publishing push until at least one long-form anchor is live and linked. Existing Shorts should receive pinned CTAs retroactively once long-form publishes.

### Phase 2 — Shorts funnel (Weeks 3–8, overlapping Phase 1)

Resume and systematize Shorts as discovery engine, now with a destination.

| Cadence | Target | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| 1 Short per week | 4–6 Shorts in first funnel cycle | Story-hook pattern (proven 959-view performer), not date teasers |
| Each Short | Pinned comment + end card → long-form anchor | Test H3 from research report |
| Title convention | Always include "Oskar Corbenn" | Disambiguation |

Content specifics (hooks, visuals, captions) are Content Engine outputs at G4–G6 — strategy defines format role only.

### Phase 3 — DSP optimization (conditional, Weeks 4–8)

Activate when Spotify/Apple presence is confirmed.

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Verify artist page links from YouTube description and pinned comments | Cross-platform graph |
| Submit playlist pitches (editorial + independent Spanish emotional rock playlists) | Passive discovery for 25–45 |
| Add Spotify Canvas if platform supports it | Visual identity extension |

### Phase 4 — Secondary platform expansion (conditional, Week 8+)

Activate Instagram Reels only after:
1. Long-form anchor live ≥2 weeks
2. Handle confirmed
3. YouTube funnel showing measurable Short → long-form CTR

Repurpose YouTube long-form and Short assets to Reels — do not create platform-native content from scratch until repurposing pipeline is proven.

## Options

Three strategic approaches evaluated against research evidence, Brand DNA, campaign constraints, and resource reality.

### Option A — YouTube Hub First (Shorts → Long-form funnel)

**Approach:** Concentrate 90% of effort on YouTube for 8 weeks. Fix infrastructure (OAC, naming, cross-links), publish long-form anchor, then systematize Shorts funnel with CTAs. Defer all secondary platforms until funnel metrics prove conversion.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Strengths** | Directly addresses verified #1 barrier (no long-form destination); leverages proven Shorts traction; matches research recommendation; lowest resource strain; highest confidence |
| **Weaknesses** | Slower DSP/stream growth if Spotify is live but unlinked; no Instagram presence during initial window |
| **Brand DNA fit** | Strong — depth-first storytelling, no trend-chasing |
| **Resource fit** | Strong — single-platform focus realistic for early-stage project |
| **Expected outcome** | Measurable subscriber and watch time growth; foundation for album-era recognition |
| **Timeline** | Long-form anchor within 4 weeks; funnel metrics by week 8 |

### Option B — Simultaneous Multi-Platform Push

**Approach:** Publish on YouTube, Instagram Reels, and TikTok concurrently from Week 1. Create platform-native content for each. Pursue Spotify playlist pitching immediately regardless of verification status.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Strengths** | Maximum surface area; faster potential reach if all platforms are live |
| **Weaknesses** | Research confirms TikTok absent and Instagram unverified; splits limited resources across unproven channels; violates research recommendation to fix YouTube hub first; high risk of inconsistent quality and identity fragmentation |
| **Brand DNA fit** | Weak — quantity over depth; risks generic content to fill cadence |
| **Resource fit** | Poor — 18 platforms listed in metadata but verified activity on one |
| **Expected outcome** | Likely shallow engagement across many platforms; no platform reaches conversion threshold |
| **Timeline** | Immediate activity but no funnel architecture |

### Option C — Album-Era Narrative Depth (slow build)

**Approach:** Prioritize long-form story content and album framing (*Razones Para Quedarme* Chapter 1) over Shorts volume. Publish one long-form piece every 2–3 weeks (MV, story video, lyric video). Minimal Shorts (1 every 2 weeks). No secondary platforms for 12 weeks.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Strengths** | Highest alignment with Noche Cero / Blucer comparable models; deepest brand trust building; lowest policy risk (no mass Shorts production) |
| **Weaknesses** | Slower discovery — abandons proven Shorts traction (959 views); may underperform discoverability KPI in 8-week window; risks passive visibility remaining near baseline |
| **Brand DNA fit** | Very strong — quality and storytelling prioritized |
| **Resource fit** | Moderate — fewer assets but higher production bar per piece |
| **Expected outcome** | High trust per viewer but low total reach; better suited as Phase 2 refinement than launch strategy |
| **Timeline** | Meaningful metrics unlikely before week 10–12 |

## Recommendation

**Adopt Option A — YouTube Hub First (Shorts → long-form funnel)** as the primary strategy, incorporating one element from Option C: the **album-era framing** (*Razones Para Quedarme*, Chapter 1) embedded in all long-form and channel branding.

### Why Option A

1. **Evidence-led:** YouTube is the only verified platform with artist-owned content and measurable traction. Research confidence for YouTube baseline is 88% — highest of all platforms.
2. **Closes the critical gap:** The verified #1 barrier is the absence of a long-form conversion destination. Option A sequences infrastructure → long-form → Shorts funnel in the order research supports.
3. **Protects resources:** Multi-platform sprawl (Option B) before hub architecture exists is explicitly flagged as a risk in the research report. Option A maximizes ROI on limited resources.
4. **Balances discovery and trust:** Unlike Option C, Option A preserves the proven Shorts discovery channel while adding the depth layer comparables use for conversion.
5. **Testable:** Research hypotheses H1–H4 map directly to Option A's execution plan and KPI table.

### Why not Option B or C alone

- **Option B** contradicts verified evidence (TikTok absent, Instagram unverified) and campaign constraints (consistent quality over quantity). Resource strain makes it unexecutable at current project stage.
- **Option C alone** underutilizes the strongest verified asset (959-view story-hook Short) and risks missing the 8-week discoverability window defined in the Campaign Brief.

### Recommended hybrid execution summary

```
Phase 0 (Week 1)     → Infrastructure: naming, OAC, cross-links, dashboard access
Phase 1 (Weeks 2–4)  → Long-form anchor + story-behind-song (Option C framing)
Phase 2 (Weeks 3–8)  → Shorts funnel with CTAs to long-form (Option A engine)
Phase 3 (Conditional) → DSP optimization when Spotify verified
Phase 4 (Conditional) → Instagram Reels repurposing when funnel CTR ≥3%
```

**Explicit deferrals:**
- TikTok: no activation until YouTube funnel operational (minimum 4 weeks post long-form)
- Paid advertising: not recommended at this stage — organic funnel must prove conversion before spend
- Viral trend participation: excluded per Campaign Brief constraints
