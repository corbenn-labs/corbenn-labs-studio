## Objective

Reduce uncertainty so Corbenn Labs can decide how to increase discoverability, audience growth, and meaningful engagement for Oskar Corbenn through *El Cambio de Tono* — without sacrificing authenticity or pursuing short-term viral tactics.

This report answers the five open questions defined in `campaign-brief.md`, using verified project documentation and independently collected platform evidence.

---

## Executive Summary

*El Cambio de Tono* is **released and partially discoverable**, but the project remains in an **early, fragmented digital state** seven days after release (2026-06-20).

**Verified presence today:**
- YouTube channel **Oskar Corbenn** publishes Shorts (best-performing Short: **959 views**, 8 likes).
- YouTube **Topic auto-upload** hosts the full track (**89 views**, 10 likes) via DistroKid under label *Razones Para Quedarme*.
- **YouTube Music** lists the song (3:54, artist Oskar Corbenn).

**Not verified or absent:**
- **Spotify** and **Apple Music** artist/track pages were not found via independent search despite distribution being listed in project metadata.
- **TikTok:** no profile or content found under Oskar Corbenn.
- **Instagram:** unable to verify (no indexed profile confirmed).
- **Official Artist Channel (OAC):** not present — music lives on auto-generated Topic channel, not a unified artist hub.

**Greatest platform opportunity:** **YouTube** — the only platform with verified artist-owned content, emotional Shorts copy aligned with Brand DNA, and campaign metrics (views, watch time) already defined. YouTube Music extends streaming discovery within the same ecosystem.

**Greatest discovery barriers:** (1) extremely low view counts and no algorithmic momentum; (2) split identity between Topic channel and artist channel; (3) no official long-form music video on the artist channel; (4) title/name collision with unrelated "Cambio de Tono" content; (5) weak or unverified cross-platform linking; (6) `Content Status: Pending` in metadata suggests post-release marketing assets may be incomplete.

**Overall confidence: 72%** — sufficient to inform Phase 3 with documented gaps. Leadership should supply distributor dashboard access (Spotify/Apple stream counts) and confirm Instagram/TikTok handles before finalizing numeric targets.

### Question 4 — Which content formats produce the highest engagement for this niche?

**Evidence tier:** Industry/platform documentation + verified Oskar Corbenn Short performance + comparable practices. No controlled A/B test data exists for Oskar Corbenn specifically.

#### Format performance hierarchy (for emotional/cinematic rock, independent artists)

| Format | Engagement role | Evidence | Fit for *El Cambio de Tono* |
|--------|-------------------|----------|----------------------------|
| **YouTube Shorts — emotional hook / lyric tease** | Discovery; highest current views for Oskar Corbenn (959 on storm metaphor Short) | Verified channel data; Chartlex/Tools4Music 2026: Shorts feed is separate algorithm, optimized for completion rate | **Strong** — "Hay tormentas que comienzan mucho antes de la lluvia" mirrors story.md |
| **Long-form music video / visualizer** | Subscriber conversion; watch time; satisfaction signals | YouTube Engineering blog: watchtime + valued watchtime drive recommendations; Chartlex: long-form converts to engaged fans ~3× vs Shorts-only | **Critical gap** — no artist-channel long-form MV verified; Topic upload has 89 views |
| **"Story behind the song" long-form** | Depth; comment engagement; brand trust | Symphonic Latino 2026; Alan Spicer Shorts funnel: long-form is "audition" after Short | **Strong** — rich source material in `story.md` |
| **Lyric video / videolyric** | Lower production cost; extends release window | Noche Cero uses videolyrics; Blucer one video per track | **Moderate** — supports album era |
| **Streaming-native (Spotify Canvas, playlist pitching)** | Passive discovery for 25–45 | Spotify LATAM playlist identity behavior (La Nación 2023) | **Unknown** — Spotify presence unverified |
| **Instagram Reels** | Visual mood; save/share behavior | Industry standard for pre-release teasers (Noche Cero model) | **Potential** — unverified account |
| **TikTok** | Viral discovery; younger skew | No Oskar presence; less aligned with 25–45 cinematic rock | **Low priority** until YouTube funnel works |

#### Why Shorts alone underperform for campaign goals

Industry sources converge (Chartlex 2026; Tools4Music 2026; ThumbMentor 2026):
- Shorts and long-form run on **separate algorithms** — a viral Short does not automatically promote long-form content.
- Only ~**10% audience overlap** between Shorts viewers and long-form viewers without deliberate funnel design.
- Long-form viewers convert to subscribers at **8–15%** vs passive Shorts traffic.
- Campaign success metrics include **watch time** and **streams** — both require long-form or DSP surfaces, not Shorts alone.

**Verified micro-evidence:** Oskar Corbenn's best Short (959 views, emotional Spanish hook, cinematic rain metaphor) outperforms other Shorts (76, 11 views) — suggesting **story-first Shorts** resonate more than date-only teasers. This supports but does not prove format superiority; sample size is too small (n=3 Shorts).

## Observations

*(Pattern inferences — not verified causal claims.)*

1. **Pre-release Shorts outperformed post-release Topic upload** (959 vs 89 views). The audience may respond more to **narrative hooks** than to a static auto-upload — or pre-release timing captured different distribution.

2. **The storm/rain metaphor Short aligns tightly with Brand DNA and story.md** — visual and lyrical identity may be the strongest discovery asset, not the genre label "Dark Soul."

3. **Distribution through DistroKid is working at the infrastructure level** (Topic upload confirms delivery) but **not yet at the discovery level** (no visible Spotify graph, no OAC).

4. **Campaign is correctly scoped for post-release** — seven days is too early for organic breakout, but long enough to confirm that **passive distribution alone is insufficient** for the stated business goals.

5. **Competitors reaching similar audiences do not depend on virality** — they depend on **chapter-based storytelling and platform hubs**. Oskar Corbenn has the story (`story.md`) but not yet the hub architecture comparables use.

## Opportunities

1. **YouTube Shorts → long-form funnel** — Verified best Short proves emotional hooks attract views; industry evidence supports converting Shorts viewers to long-form with deliberate CTAs (pinned comments, related video links).

2. **Story-behind-the-song content** — `story.md` contains production-ready narrative (intuition, rain, marriage, subtle shift) that comparables use for depth and cannot be replicated by Topic auto-uploads.

3. **Album-era framing (*Razones Para Quedarme*)** — Positions *El Cambio de Tono* as Chapter 1, reducing one-single cold-start problem — matches Noche Cero and Blucer album-concept models.

4. **Cinematic visual identity differentiation** — Dark, rain, night aesthetics distinguish from generic AI music farms targeted by YouTube's inauthentic-content policy (YouTube Help, Jul 2025).

5. **DSP confirmation window** — Release is 7 days old; Spotify/Apple indexing may lag. Leadership distributor dashboard could unlock playlist and stream data for Phase 3 targeting.

6. **Honest AI-assistance positioning** — Comparables gain press and trust through transparency; aligns with Master Context vision of "respected AI-assisted independent artist."

## Recommendation

*(For leadership before authorizing G3 — not marketing strategy.)*

1. **Approve G3 conditionally** — Evidence supports direction (YouTube-first, story-driven, long-form gap identified) at **72% confidence**. Request leadership provide **DistroKid/distributor analytics** (Spotify/Apple streams, territories) and **confirmed social handles** to raise confidence before numeric targets are finalized.

2. **Instruct Marketing Strategist to treat YouTube as primary discovery surface** — Only verified platform with artist-owned content and campaign-aligned metrics.

3. **Flag long-form music video (or cinematic visualizer) as the highest-priority content gap** — Topic upload cannot serve as the campaign's storytelling anchor; `Content Status: Pending` in metadata aligns with this finding.

4. **Require OAC / channel consolidation analysis in Phase 3** — Split Topic vs artist channel is a verified discovery barrier.

5. **Mandate artist-name disambiguation in all metadata** — Always "Oskar Corbenn — El Cambio de Tono" to reduce title collision with SonAMA and generic results.

6. **Do not expand to TikTok until YouTube funnel architecture exists** — Verified absent; audience fit weaker than YouTube for 25–45 cinematic rock.

7. **Use Noche Cero and Blucer as reference models, not vanity benchmarks** — Their documented success factors are narrative coherence and platform hubs; their exact metrics were not verified in this cycle.
