# Corbenn Labs Operating Principles

## Purpose

This document defines the fundamental operating principles of Corbenn Labs.

These principles guide every workflow, specialist, and business decision across the system.

Unlike workflows or agent definitions, these principles should change rarely.

They represent architectural decisions validated through real execution.

---

# Principle 1 — Workflow Before Agents

The workflow is the foundation of Corbenn Labs.

Agents may evolve, improve, or even be replaced.

The workflow defines how work moves through the system.

---

# Principle 2 — One Gate, One Deliverable

Every workflow gate exists to produce one primary deliverable.

A gate is complete only after its expected deliverable has been reviewed and approved.

---

# Principle 3 — Specialists Own Expertise

Each specialist is responsible only for its own domain.

No specialist should perform another specialist's responsibilities.

Expertise is distributed intentionally.

---

# Principle 4 — The CEO Orchestrates

The CEO coordinates work.

The CEO does not replace specialist knowledge.

Leadership assigns gates, reviews deliverables, and authorizes progression through the workflow.

---

# Principle 5 — Evidence Before Strategy

Strategic decisions must be supported by research whenever possible.

Assumptions should never replace verified evidence.

Uncertainty must be documented.

---

# Principle 6 — Strategy Before Production

Creative production begins only after strategy has been approved.

Generating content without strategic direction increases inconsistency and rework.

---

# Principle 7 — Human Approval Between Gates

Human review is the quality control mechanism of Corbenn Labs.

Every workflow gate requires explicit approval before the next gate begins.

---

# Principle 8 — Single Source of Truth

Every important piece of information should have one authoritative location.

Avoid duplicated instructions or conflicting documentation.

When conflicts exist, resolve them instead of maintaining parallel versions.

---

# Principle 9 — Reuse Before Create

Existing knowledge, research, and creative assets should be reused whenever possible.

The system should maximize the value extracted from previous work before creating new assets.

---

# Principle 10 — Continuous Improvement Through Execution

The operating system improves through real projects, not theoretical design.

Architecture changes should be driven by evidence gathered during completed workflow cycles.

---

# Principle 11 — Separate Products from Process

Project folders contain business deliverables.

Cycle folders contain execution history.

Never mix project artifacts with workflow history.

---

# Principle 12 — Decisions Are Documented

Significant architectural and business decisions should be documented.

Future improvements should build upon recorded knowledge rather than memory.

---

# Principle 13 — Simplicity Over Complexity

Every document, workflow, and agent should remain as simple as possible while fulfilling its purpose.

Complexity requires justification.

---

# Principle 14 — Long-Term Thinking

Short-term optimization must never compromise the long-term integrity of the system.

Every improvement should strengthen Corbenn Labs for future projects.

---

# Principle 15 — The System Is the Product

Corbenn Labs is not fundamentally a collection of AI agents.

It is an operating system for executing creative and business workflows.

Agents are components.

The workflow is the product.
